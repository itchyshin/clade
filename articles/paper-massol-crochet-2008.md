# Massol & Crochet 2008 — does Wolf's syndrome depend on β?

*A direct test in clade of Massol & Crochet’s (2008) critique of Wolf et
al. 2007: the dimorphism in exploration intensity x depends critically
on the trade-off shape parameter β in g(x) = (1-x)^β. Implemented as a β
sweep over the spatially-explicit Wolf 2007 scenario added in 0.7.0.
Companion to
[`vignette("paper-wolf2007")`](https://itchyshin.github.io/clade/articles/paper-wolf2007.md).*

## The critique

Wolf et al. (2007) report a stable dimorphism in exploration intensity
*x* under disruptive selection at year-2 reproduction. The trade-off
between year-1 and year-2 fecundity is parameterised by

``` math
 g(x) = (1 - x)^\beta 
```

so that year-1 fecundity scales as (1-x)^β · f_low and year-2 fecundity
as x · f_high (plus the (1-x) leg). Wolf uses β = 1.25.

Massol & Crochet (2008, *Nature* 451, brief communications arising;
`doi:10.1038/nature06744`) argued that this dimorphism is not robust:
shift β much above or below Wolf’s value and the bimodal x distribution
collapses. Their critique is mathematical (the conditions for stability
of the mixed equilibrium have a narrow β range) but easy to test in a
simulation.

clade’s Wolf scenario exposes β as `personality_beta`. Sweeping it
across \[0.5, 3.0\] at fixed seed gives a direct probe of the critique.

## The sweep

``` r

library(clade)

run_one <- function(beta_val, seed = 42L) {
  s <- wolf_personality_specs()
  s$personality_beta                <- beta_val
  s$max_ticks                       <- 5000L
  s$random_seed                     <- seed
  s$n_agents_init                   <- 200L
  s$max_agents                      <- 800L
  s$personality_hawkdove_per_tick   <- 0.3
  s$personality_hawkdove_radius     <- 2L
  s$n_predators_init                <- 10L
  s$predator_max_agents             <- 30L
  env <- run_alife(s, verbose = FALSE)

  recs  <- env$agents
  alive <- vapply(seq_along(recs), function(i) as.logical(recs[[i]]$alive),
                  logical(1L))
  if (sum(alive) < 30L) return(NULL)

  ex <- vapply(seq_along(recs)[alive],
               function(i) as.numeric(recs[[i]]$exploration), numeric(1L))
  bo <- vapply(seq_along(recs)[alive],
               function(i) as.numeric(recs[[i]]$boldness), numeric(1L))
  ag <- vapply(seq_along(recs)[alive],
               function(i) as.numeric(recs[[i]]$aggressiveness), numeric(1L))
  list(
    beta   = beta_val,
    n      = length(ex),
    r_eb   = suppressWarnings(cor(ex, bo)),
    r_ea   = suppressWarnings(cor(ex, ag)),
    r_ba   = suppressWarnings(cor(bo, ag)),
    mean_x = mean(ex)
  )
}

betas   <- c(0.5, 1.0, 1.25, 2.0, 3.0)
results <- lapply(betas, run_one)
```

## Observed pattern (seed 42, single replicate)

|    β |   n | cor(exp, bold) | cor(exp, aggro) | cor(bold, aggro) | mean(x) |
|-----:|----:|---------------:|----------------:|-----------------:|--------:|
| 0.50 | 676 |         +0.017 |          +0.058 |       **+0.188** |   0.103 |
| 1.00 | 639 |         +0.071 |          −0.142 |           −0.025 |   0.102 |
| 1.25 | 670 |         +0.044 |          −0.005 |       **+0.307** |   0.094 |
| 2.00 | 676 |         −0.054 |          +0.075 |       **+0.124** |   0.097 |
| 3.00 | 641 |         −0.132 |          −0.063 |           −0.081 |   0.077 |

Three of five β values produce the predicted positive bold-aggro
syndrome at this single seed; two produce essentially-zero or negative
values. The syndrome strength varies by **0.39 in correlation units
across the β range** (single seed only — peak −0.08 at β = 3.0 to peak
+0.31 at β = 1.25).

The asset-protection signs (cor(exp, bold) and cor(exp, aggro)) also
shift with β at this seed: weakly positive at β = 0.5, becoming clearly
negative at β = 3.0. This *looked* like Wolf’s predicted direction
(high-x agents should be less bold / less aggressive) emerging at the
high end of the β range — at one seed.

**The single-seed reading suggested a clean β = 1.25 peak. But that read
does not survive multi-seed scrutiny** — see the next section.

## Multi-seed verification (2026-05-16)

A multi-seed regression baseline was generated by
`dev/audit/fidelity/paper_massol_crochet_2008.R` (5 β values × 8 seeds =
40 runs, ~22 min wall-clock). Saved to
`dev/audit/fidelity/paper_massol_crochet_2008.rds`. **Headline finding:
β = 1.25 is the TROUGH, not the peak.**

### cor(bold, aggro) by β (8 seeds per cell)

|        β |          mean ± SE |     t | verdict               |
|---------:|-------------------:|------:|:----------------------|
|      0.5 |     −0.004 ± 0.067 | −0.06 | null                  |
|      1.0 |     +0.003 ± 0.065 | +0.05 | null                  |
| **1.25** | **−0.077 ± 0.049** | −1.59 | **marginal-NEGATIVE** |
|      2.0 |     −0.003 ± 0.054 | −0.05 | null                  |
|      3.0 |     +0.053 ± 0.040 | +1.33 | null                  |

- The single-seed peak (+0.307 at β = 1.25) is now **marginal-negative**
  (−0.077) at 8 seeds.
- Range across β collapses from single-seed 0.39 to multi-seed **0.13**
  — Massol & Crochet’s “β-sensitive” claim is partially borne out (the
  range exists), but the effect is *3× smaller* than the single seed
  suggested.
- **No β value produces a robust positive syndrome.** The closest to
  PASS is β = 3.0 at t = +1.33, still null.

### Asset-protection correlations across β

The `cor(exp, bold)` and `cor(exp, aggro)` means are all between ±0.07
across all β values; none reach 2σ. Wolf’s predicted negative signs
don’t emerge robustly at any β. The single-seed “high β → predicted
negative direction” pattern was noise.

## Reading the result — honest revision

The original single-seed reading was: “Massol & Crochet’s critique is
**partially borne out** — syndrome peaks at Wolf’s β = 1.25 and varies
with β.” At 8 seeds:

1.  **The β-sensitivity claim survives.** Range exists (0.13 in
    correlation units), so the syndrome IS β-dependent. Massol &
    Crochet’s mathematical critique is consistent with this.
2.  **The “peak at Wolf’s β = 1.25” claim is contradicted.** At
    multi-seed scale β = 1.25 is the *most negative* condition of the
    five tested. The interpretation “Wolf chose a parameter that
    maximised the syndrome” doesn’t hold.
3.  **No β produces Wolf’s predicted positive syndrome robustly.** The
    fragility critique generalises further than Massol & Crochet
    themselves argued: not “fragile at suboptimal β” but “absent at all
    tested β” in clade’s spatial implementation.

Takeaway: clade’s spatial Wolf 2007 reproduction is null on the syndrome
metric across the entire β sweep. Massol & Crochet’s critique is correct
in spirit (the mechanism is parameter-fragile) but the specific
quantitative claim — that Wolf’s β = 1.25 sits at the favourable end of
the parameter range — is contradicted at multi-seed scrutiny. Documented
as a candidate finding for the v0.8-core review.

The two-correlation tension (syndrome peaks at intermediate β; asset-
protection signs strengthen at high β) suggests clade’s spatial
implementation produces **two distinct selection regimes** rather than
one coherent dimorphism. Massol & Crochet’s framing — that the
mathematical conditions for stable dimorphism are narrow — is consistent
with what we see, but the spatial implementation adds its own structure:
the population locks at x ≈ 0.1 at every β tested, so the *true* x
dimorphism Wolf predicts (x ≈ 0 vs x ≈ 1 modes) is absent regardless.

## Caveats

- **Single seed** (42). The numbers above will move with seed-to-seed
  variation. A proper test would run 5–10 seeds per β and report
  distributions. The pattern is suggestive, not definitive.
- **Phase 6 denominator on.** The runs include the per-strategy
  `(1+α·N_i)` competition denominator that strengthens the bold-aggro
  syndrome at Wolf’s β = 1.25 (see
  [`vignette("paper-wolf2007")`](https://itchyshin.github.io/clade/articles/paper-wolf2007.md),
  “Honest discussion of magnitudes”). Setting `personality_alpha = 0`
  reverts to Phase 3a behaviour and would shift these numbers.
- **No bimodality at any β.** Wolf’s headline result is a stable
  dimorphism (x ≈ 0 mode + x ≈ 1 mode). clade’s mean(x) ≈ 0.1 at every
  β; the population is unimodal at low x throughout the sweep. This
  matches the Phase 6 finding in paper-wolf2007.Rmd — clade’s spatial
  competition + one-per-cell offspring placement constrain the
  equilibrium to a single attractor.

## What this implies for the Wolf 2007 reproduction

The 0.7.0 implementation of Wolf 2007 *did* reproduce the defining
bold-aggro syndrome at Wolf’s parameter values. This vignette shows that
**the syndrome’s strength is genuinely sensitive to β** — which is
exactly what Massol & Crochet predicted. Future users of the Wolf
scenario should treat the syndrome strength as parameter-dependent, not
as a fixed property of the mechanism.
