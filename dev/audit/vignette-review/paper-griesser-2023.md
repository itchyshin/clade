## CLAIM (partial)

Griesser et al. 2023's claim: longer parental care → larger
adult brain size. clade's two-stage reproduction:

- **Stage 1 (grid search)**: Sweep `brain_size_cost_scale` to
  find a cell where brain size is under selection without
  fatal cost. **Best cost = 1.5** (lower drives runaway
  brain, higher drives collapse).
- **Stage 2 (multi-seed at best cell)**: Vary
  `care_duration`. Spearman ρ(care_duration, final_brain) =
  **+0.248** across 32 runs.

The +0.25 Spearman is *weak but in the predicted direction*.
The vignette is honest: the magnitude is smaller than the
empirical estimate Griesser et al. reported, but the
direction is robust across seeds.

## BIO

`brain_size_cost_scale = 1.5` is the calibrated knob —
brain-cost cells of 1.0 and 2.0 produced runaway or
collapse respectively (visible in `stage1_slopes`). The
audit honestly reports that the brain-size-evolution module
operates over a narrow defensible window.

The weak +0.25 Spearman is the right interpretation: with
only 4 care_durations × 8 seeds = 32 runs, a weak positive
correlation is real but not decisive. Griesser et al.
reported a larger effect size in real data; the gap may
reflect calibration, missing mechanism, or the difference
between empirical (lifetime) and simulated (per-tick) brain
measurements. Vignette acknowledges the gap.
